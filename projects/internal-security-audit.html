Internal Security Audit

Comprehensive cybersecurity audit for Botium Toys using NIST Cybersecurity Framework, evaluating controls and compliance with PCI DSS, GDPR, and SOC standards

Timeline

Oct 2025

Client

Botium Toys

Framework

NIST Cybersecurity Framework

Role

Security Auditor

Project Overview

Conducted a comprehensive internal IT security audit for Botium Toys, a small U.S. toy retailer expanding its online operations. The audit evaluated existing administrative, technical, and physical controls, assessed compliance with PCI DSS, GDPR, and SOC frameworks, and provided actionable recommendations to improve the company's security posture.

Company Context

Botium Toys develops and sells toys through a physical store and online platform. As the company grows internationally, risks around compliance, data protection, and system reliability have increased. The IT department manages employee devices, internal systems, customer data, and online payment systems.

Audit Scope

The audit covered the following asset groups:

  • On-premises and employee equipment (computers, mobile devices, network hardware)
  • Internal network and data storage systems
  • Customer payment processing systems
  • Cloud services and internet access
  • Physical facilities (storefront, warehouse, offices)

Controls Assessment Results

Access Controls

❌ Least Privilege - All employees have access to internal and cardholder data

❌ Separation of Duties - Not implemented; broad access across teams

⚠️ Password Policies - Exists but not compliant with modern standards

Network Security

✅ Firewall - Configured to filter and block unwanted traffic

❌ Intrusion Detection - Not yet installed

✅ Antivirus Software - Installed and monitored regularly

Data Protection

❌ Encryption - Customer credit card data not encrypted

❌ Backups - Critical data not backed up

❌ Password Management - No centralized password manager

Physical Security

✅ Physical Locks - Offices, storefront, and warehouse secured

✅ CCTV Surveillance - Operational and monitored

✅ Fire Detection - Fire alarms and sprinklers active

Compliance Assessment

PCI DSS Compliance

  • ❌ Authorized users only access cardholder data
  • ❌ Credit card data processed/stored securely
  • ❌ Encryption procedures implemented
  • ⚠️ Secure password management policies

GDPR Compliance

  • ⚠️ EU customers' data kept private/secured
  • ✅ 72-hour breach notification plan
  • ❌ Data classification and inventory
  • ✅ Privacy policy enforcement

SOC 1 / SOC 2 Compliance

  • ❌ User access policies established
  • ❌ Sensitive data remains confidential/private
  • ✅ Data integrity maintained
  • ✅ Data available to authorized users

Key Recommendations

  1. Implement Access Control Policies - Introduce least privilege and separation of duties with role-based access control (RBAC)
  2. Adopt Encryption for Sensitive Data - Encrypt credit card data and personal information both at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+)
  3. Develop Disaster Recovery and Backup Plan - Automate daily backups, store offsite, and test recovery quarterly
  4. Deploy Intrusion Detection System (IDS) - Add IDS/IPS for real-time network monitoring and alerting
  5. Upgrade Password Management and MFA - Enforce complex passwords, integrate password manager, and require multi-factor authentication
  6. Establish Asset Classification and Inventory - Maintain centralized catalog of all devices, systems, and data for visibility and compliance
  7. Enhance Employee Security Awareness - Conduct quarterly training on data handling, phishing prevention, and privacy compliance

Technologies & Frameworks Used

NIST Cybersecurity Framework PCI DSS GDPR SOC 1/2 Risk Assessment Compliance Auditing Security Controls GRC

Project Impact

This internal audit revealed several critical gaps in access control, encryption, and disaster recovery procedures. The comprehensive assessment provided Botium Toys with a clear roadmap to achieve stronger compliance with PCI DSS, GDPR, and SOC standards while improving overall resilience and reducing business risk.

Key Lessons Learned

This project demonstrated the importance of systematic security auditing in identifying vulnerabilities before they become incidents. The NIST Cybersecurity Framework provided an excellent structure for evaluating controls across all domains, while compliance assessment revealed the interconnected nature of security, privacy, and business operations. The experience highlighted how small businesses often have significant security gaps that can be addressed through systematic implementation of controls and regular monitoring.
